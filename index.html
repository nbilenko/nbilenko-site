Dr. Natalia Bilenko

I am a machine learning engineer, experienced with image and video data (scientific and medical), predictive modeling, and data visualization. I am particularly interested in algorithm interpretability and human-computer interaction. My goal is to create algorithms and applications that are more equitable and are designed to serve humans that they impact the most.

From 2016 to 2019, I worked as a machine learning engineer at Caption Health (formerly Bay Labs), designing algorithms and software for detection and diagnosis of heart disease based on ultrasound imaging. I cofounded the diversity and inclusion working group and helped start the internship program at the company. I also managed the global outreach program, through which we collaborated with the nonprofit organization Team Heart in Rwanda.

Before that, I received a PhD from the Helen Wills Neuroscience Institute at UC Berkeley, where I was supervised by Jack Gallant (yep, that's a video of my brain). For my disseration, I modeled similarities across people's brain responses to movies and speech to study how different kinds of information are represented in the brain. While at Berkeley, I received a Masters in computer science, advised by Maneesh Agrawala. My thesis project was a web tool for creating custom visualizations of texts that reflected character relationships and emotional trajectories.

Advocacy

I volunteer for Code for America: Open Oakland. We are building a website and API for air quality data in support of the West Oakland Environmental Indicators Project, a community-based environmental justice organization.

I am an organizer for Queer in AI, a group working to raise awareness of queer issues in machine learning and to forge a community of queer researchers. In December 2019, we hosted a workshop at NeurIPS 2019 in Vancouver, where I moderated an expert panel on algorithmic inequity and its impacts on marginalized groups. Most recently, we hosted a social event at ACM FAT* 2020 conference. Please feel free to email me if you'd like to get involved.

Art and design

I love scientific art and design, and I'm starting to work on a new collaborative art project - more on this soon!

I am co-leading a workshop at the CtrlZ.AI Zine Fair in association with the ACM FAT* 2020 conference called Our Relationship with AI: Exploring the Present + Dreaming Up Radical Futures in collaboration with some brilliant and creative folks: Ria Kalluri, Michelle Carney, Rapha Gontijo Lopes, and Elliot Creager. If you'd like to participate in an asynchronous part of our workshop, please take this survey about your casual encounters with AI.

I was part of the team that created Dr. Brainlove, an interactive climbable brain sculpture with EEG-based programmable lights. Dr. Brainlove went to Burning Man twice and participated in the Bay Area Science Festival, and I moderated a panel on Dr. Brainlove at South by Southwest Interactive.

For an exhibition at the Exploratorium on cognitive technologies, I co-developed the Mind's Eye, an installation for interactively exploring predictive modeling and decoding of brain activity via motion and gesture sensing, with James Gao.

As a graduate student at Berkeley, I was a designer for the magazine Berkeley Science Review and led development for our first mobile app.
